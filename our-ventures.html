What is in a name?

The Internet society has developed a global culture with a specific language: Internet English (IE). One of the features of IE is an exceptionally fast rate of word creation. An example of this is the use of the affix -preneur in compounds such as Womenpreneur, Homepreneur, Mompreneur, Blackpreneur, Youngpreneur and other variations, to designate a certain type of expertise. Now, is this slew of neologisms really necessary? For their creators the answer is a resonant yes. Why? Because what they are striving to achieve are specificity and recognition.

In other words, even if they do not know anything about Targeting and Differentiation, that is what they want. These preneurs feel that whatever they are offering to customers, whether it is a certain knowledge, a new concept or a product, it is useful to at least some of them (their target audience). That is the key. To make your offer relevant to your target audience it is mandatory to make it stand out from your competitors’. To do this, you create a name or a brand that your audience can relate to, both as a brand image and as a brand experience. Whenever possible, that name should convey a sense of uniqueness for your expertise, concept or product. You must create a perceived value that is worth the customers’ attention, time, and eventually, money.
